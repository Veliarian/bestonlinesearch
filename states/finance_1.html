Debt Consolidation Loans: What Are They & Are They Good for You?

A debt consolidation loan is a type of personal loan that rolls your high-interest debts into one low-interest monthly payment.

These Loans Can Be Used to Pay Off Unsecured Debts, Including:

– Credit card bills

– Medical bills

– Personal and payday loans

Taking out a debt consolidation loan can give people overwhelmed by debt the breathing room they need to get back on track with their repayments. And with interest rates at record lows, it’s even easier now to pay off debt with these loans than making minimum payments on credit cards.

How Do They Work?

If you have multiple credit cards with high rates of interest, the accumulating charges can make paying off your balances difficult.

A debt consolidation loan will help you reduce the cost of your total debt and reorganize it so you can pay it off quicker. With the loan, you can pay off your creditors or use a lender that sends money straight to your creditors. Once that is done, all you have to do is pay back the loan in installments (at lower interest) over a fixed term.

Debt Consolidation Loans Are a Good Idea If

They help you save on interest: If you have high-interest debt, a low-interest debt consolidation loan will help you save money. Also, unlike credit cards, loans have to be paid off over a fixed term – this gives you an end date for your debt.

They result in a lower monthly payment: A debt consolidation loan will reorganize and spread out your debt payments over several years, making it easier to make payments on time.

They improve your credit score. Taking out a new loan and leaving consolidated accounts open (but unused) will result in a lower credit utilization ratio. This, along with a history of on-time payments, can boost your credit score.
