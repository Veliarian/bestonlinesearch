These Are The Most Common Types Of Cancer

The earlier cancer can be found by the doctors, the sooner the treatment can start. Therefore, it is important to know the most common types of cancer and their warning signs. While there are more than 100 different types of cancer, some are more common than others, and depend on certain factors like your gender, racial group, age, or ethnic group. For example, breast cancer is more likely to occur in women, and prostate cancer only affects men. 

Listed below are the common types of cancer with their warning signs:

Non-Melanoma Skin Cancer

This most common type of cancer includes squamous cell carcinoma and basal cell carcinoma. Each year, it affects about more than a million Americans. 

Signs of squamous cell carcinoma include:

– Wart-like growths

– Growths that are painful, itchy, or irritated

– Red, scaly patches with uneven borders

– Sores that won’t heal, bleed easily, or that form a crust that won’t go away

Signs of basal cell carcinoma include:

– Scaly, red, raised patches

– Sores that heal and then reappear or that won’t heal

– Flat, pale areas of the skin that look like scars

– Pink growths with indents or raised edges

– Growths or sores that itch, bleed, or have small blood vessels on their surface

– Smooth, shiny, small lumps that are white, red, or pink

Breast Cancer:

The warning signs of breast cancer include:

– Swelling in your breast

– Pain in your nipples or breasts

– A new mass or lump in your armpit, breast, or around the collarbone. While most lumps are painless, some might be tender or painful. 

– Redness, irritation, thickening, dimpling (which might make your skin look like an orange peel), or scaliness of the skin on your breast

– Nipple retraction (a nipple that’s turned inward or “dented”)

– Nipple discharge that is not breast milk

If you see any changes in your breasts, it is recommended to consult your doctor or a breast specialist. 

Lung Cancer:

Some of the early signs of lung cancer are as follows:

– Coughing up blood

– Bone pain

– Constant chest pain

– A persistent cough that won’t go away and worsens over time

– Shortness of breath or wheezing

– Voice changes like hoarseness

– Losing weight without trying

– Blood clots

– Not feeling hungry

– Regular lung infections like bronchitis or pneumonia

– Frequent headaches

It is advised to consult a doctor if you see any of the above symptoms and get tested for lung cancer and other possible illnesses, like asthma.
